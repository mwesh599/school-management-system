About Us

Since its establishment in 1981, JKUAT has been dedicated to providing high-quality education to students. Our mission is to set trends in higher education, research, innovation, and entrepreneurship. We strive to be a university of global excellence, contributing to development through education and innovation.

Vision

Our vision is to be a leading institution in training, research, innovation, and entrepreneurship. We aim to make a significant impact on local and global development.

History

JKUAT is situated in Juja, approximately 36 kilometers northeast of Nairobi, along the Nairobi-Thika Highway. It was founded as a Middle Level College (known as Jomo Kenyatta College of Agriculture and Technology, or JKCAT) in 1981 with generous assistance from the Japanese Government. The first group of students was admitted on May 4, 1981. H.E. Daniel Arap Moi officially opened JKCAT on March 17, 1982. The first graduation ceremony took place in April 1984, awarding Diploma Certificates in Agricultural Engineering, Food Technology, and Horticulture. In 1988, JKCAT became a constituent college of Kenyatta University. The name was changed to Jomo Kenyatta University College of Agriculture and Technology (JKUCAT). Finally, in 1994, JKUAT was established as a university through the JKUAT Act and inaugurated on December 7, 1994.
